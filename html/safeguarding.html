Policies

For each child enrolled, Lexden Montessori will ensure:

  • that he is not subject to abuse
  • his right to privacy and confidentiality
  • all his other legal rights

Lexden Montessori will:

  • be equipped to recognise any violations of the above
  • work with guardians, the community, and external bodies, where appropriate, to ensure the above
  • not rely on unconditional trust
  • build a 'culture of safety' in which children are protected from abuse
  • Respond promptly to all incidents or concerns of abuse that may occur in accordance with What to do if you’re Worried a Child is Being Abused
  • promote staff awareness of abuse issues through training

Procedures

A Team Member is hired

When a new Team Member is hired, The Safeguarding Officer (Siobhan Brine) requires:

  • 'enhanced' disclosure checks with the Criminal Records Bureau, and
  • a minimum of two written references from
    • a previous employer, or
    • an educational establishment.

The Safeguarding Officer (Siobhan Brine) allows the applicant to challenge the information.

Observing Team Members

For each Volunteer, at least one Staff Member is present with them at any time.

A visit is booked

The Staff Member taking the booking records the following details in The Diary:

  • Name of guardian
  • Contact details for the guardian
  • Name, age, and sex of child
  • Date and time of booked visit

A visitor arrives

When a visitor V announces his arrival at The Setting via the intercom,

  1. A Staff Member S ensures that
    • V can claim, via the intercom system, without hesitation or back-tracking, a legitimate reason for visiting; and
    • Either:
      • A Staff Member recognizes V as a Child Collector of a Child booked in at that time, by comparison with photos in the Enrolment Form for that Child. In this case, V is a Visiting Child Collector.
      • V can establish his identity against an entry in The Diary. In this case, V is a Guest.
  2. If V satisfies the requirements:
    1. V signs in:
      • if V is a Visiting Child Collector, on the Arrivals and Departures Sheets.
      • if V is a Guest, in the Visitors' Book, with the purpose of the visit.
    2. V is accompanied by at least one Staff Member throughout the visit.
    3. V signs out in the same document as that on which V signed in.
  3. If V does not satisfy the requirements, S denies him entry.

A child makes a disclosure

When a Team Member T is the recipient of a disclosure by a child C,

  1. T listens to C, without
    • displaying a reaction,
    • challenging C's disclosure, or
    • questioning C.
  2. T reassures C that
    • T will (and is required to) take action,
    • only people that need to know will be told,
    • it was not C's fault,
    • speaking was the right thing to do.
  3. T follows procedures A Team Member suspects abuse.

A Team Member suspects abuse

When a Team Member T suspects that a child C is a victim of abuse, T will

  1. note down, as soon as possible, on an Incident Sheet:
    • the date and time of the observation
    • any reasons for suspicion, from the list under Indicators of Abuse, if necessary using a Body Diagram Sheet
    • any exact words spoken by C
    • the names of any other person present
    • the name and signature of T
    • the date and time of the entry
  2. pass the Incident Sheet to the The Safeguarding Officer (Siobhan Brine), after which Determining Abuse will be followed.
  3. if necessary, seeks support.

Determining Abuse

When The Safeguarding Officer (Siobhan Brine) is approached by a Team Member T with a suspicion that a child C is a victim of abuse by some suspect S, he will:

  1. Ensure that at least one Staff Member is present with S in Lexden Montessori at all times.
  2. open a file in the Child Protection Record, recording
    • Details of C, T, and S
    • the date and time he was approached
    • the date and time he noted down these details
  3. regard the changes as evidence of abuse, unless and only unless:
    • C has not been placed on the Safeguarding Children Register, and
    • immediate, clear, and consistent innocent explanation for the changes is given, without any prompting, by
      • Staff Members to all Team Members at large, or
      • C's Guardians to any Staff Member.
  4. if S is a Team Member, follow the procedures under An allegation is made against a Team Member.

If the The Safeguarding Officer (Siobhan Brine) finds evidence of abuse, he will follow the procedures under Reporting evidence of abuse. Otherwise, the file in the Child Protection Record is marked as closed, with the reasons why so.

Reporting evidence of abuse

Parents of a suspected victim of abuse are only informed of this suspicion if the Local Safeguarding Children Board gives permission for this.

When The Safeguarding Officer (Siobhan Brine) has found evidence of abuse of child C, he should:

  1. Record in the Child Protection Record under C's file that the indicators of abuse are being treated as evidence of abuse, and why.
  2. Contact the Local Safeguarding Children Board verbally.
  3. Inform all appropriate Team Members that contact has been made with the Local Safeguarding Children Board concerning C.
  4. Within 48 hours of the above contact, securely send complete copies of C's file in the Child Protection Record to the Local Safeguarding Children Board.
  5. Co-operate fully with the Local Safeguarding Children Board and/or the police or another agency identified by the Local Safeguarding Children Board in any subsequent investigation.

When Team Members are informed by the The Safeguarding Officer (Siobhan Brine) that contact has been made with the Local Safeguarding Children Board, they should take care not to influence the outcome either through the way we speak to children or by asking questions of children.

An allegation is made against a Team Member

When The Safeguarding Officer (Siobhan Brine) is approached by anyone with an accusation that a Team Member T is a perpetrator of abuse, he will:

  1. Refer it immediately to:
    • the Children's Safeguarding Service on 01245 436744
    • Ofsted
    • if the accusee is The Setting Manager (Gaynor Sloan): Children Schools and Families
  2. Request of The Setting Manager (Gaynor Sloan) that T is suspended, on the same pay rate, from entering Lexden Montessori, until the investigation is complete.
  3. Seek advice from Children Schools and Families.

Upon completion of investigation

If T is found innocent, The Safeguarding Officer (Siobhan Brine) requests of The Setting Manager (Gaynor Sloan) that T is immediately reinstated in their original post.

Internet safety

We seek parental permission before using photographs of children or their work on the nursery’s website, online media or in newsletters and other publications.

Definitions

Indicators of Abuse

Abuse

Abuse can take different forms. We recognise:

  • physical abuse, as defined below
  • emotional abuse, as defined below
  • sexual abuse, as defined below
  • neglect, as defined below
Indicator
A fact about a child which suggests that the child has been, or is, subject to abuse.
Sign
A physical, visual, apparent indicator.
Signal
A behavioural indicator.

Team Members regard the following as sufficient for suspicion of abuse.

Type of Abuse Definition Signs Signals
Physical abuse

Any kind of physical harm inflicted (with the exception of sexual abuse as defined below), which may be:

  • hitting, punching, kicking, &c.
  • biting
  • burning or scalding
  • suffocating or drowning
  • shaking or throwing
  • poisoning
  • bruises
  • scalds or burns
  • poor achievement
  • fractures
  • aggression
  • behavioural problems
Emotional abuse Persistent ill-treatment of a child such as to cause severe and persistent adverse effects on the child's emotional development.
  • the parent's treatment of the child
  • a lack of sense of self
Sexual abuse Forcing or enticing a child to take part in sexual activities (physical, visual, or mental), whether or not the child is aware of what is happening.

More visible when penetrative acts have taken place:

  • genital or anal soreness
  • abrasions
  • infections
  • pain
  • Sexualised behaviour or a knowledge thereof beyond the typical
  • Sadness, depression
  • Aggression
Neglect The persistent failure to meet a child's basic physical and/or psychological needs.
  • Guardians failing to provide:
    • food
    • clothing
    • shelter
    • medical treatment
    • for the child's emotional needs
  • Impaired growth and development
  • Poor health
  • Difficulties in forming relationships
  • Low educational achievement
Common to all  
  • physical harm
  • loss of appetite
  • disturbing content in role-play or artistic expression
  • disclosure

Enforcement

  • Prospective Visitors are informed of our procedures under A visitor arrives when they arrive at Lexden Montessori.
  • The maximum period of time between Staff Members receiving refresher training courses is six months for the online safeguarding courses and three years for the Level 2.
